The Genome STRiP CNV Browser

This CNV Browser application was developed to support the sharing, browsing, curation and quality control of copy number variation analysis done with Genome STRiP. The Genome STRiP software was developed in the lab of Steve McCarroll at Harvard Medical School, the Broad Institute and the Stanley Center for Psychiatric Research.

The principal developers of Genome STRiP are Bob Handsaker and Seva Kashin, with additional contributions from Josh Korn, David Kulp, Christina Usher, Chris Whelan and Alec Wysoker.

CNV Browser developers:
Seva Kashin
Christina Usher

The CNV Browser utilizes the Shiny framework from RStudio (www.rstudio.com).
